# Pokémon Art Design Guidelines

## 1. Core Concept
The character illustrations should focus **only on the Pokémon itself**. They serve as the central artwork for the cards, where all stats, borders, and UI elements will be handled independently by the game's CSS/HTML rendering.

## 2. Style: "Q-Version" (Chibi / Cute)
- **Proportions**: Exaggerated, cute proportions (larger heads, smaller bodies, big expressive eyes).
- **Vibe**: Friendly, playful, and approachable.
- **Complexity**: Simplified details. Avoid hyper-realism or overly gritty textures. Use clean, smooth shapes.

## 3. Composition & Layout
- **No Borders or Frames**: The image must NOT contain any painted borders, card frames, or UI elements.
- **No Text or Numbers**: Absolutely no stats (HP, attack damage), names, or text in the image itself.
- **Background**: A very simple, soft, semi-transparent or subtle background that matches the Pokémon's type (e.g., soft glow of embers for fire, gentle water ripples for water), or just a clean transparent-like backdrop so it blends perfectly when placed inside the HTML card element. The focus should remain 90% on the character.
- **Centering**: The character should be perfectly centered, occupying most of the canvas without being cropped.

## 4. Type-Specific Themes
- **Fire (e.g., Charmander, Charmeleon)**: Warm, soft lighting with tiny glowing embers.
- **Water (e.g., Squirtle, Wartortle)**: Cool, refreshing lighting with subtle water droplets or bubbles.
- **Grass (e.g., Bulbasaur, Ivysaur)**: Natural, earthy lighting with gentle leaves or a soft green glow.
- **Electric (e.g., Pikachu, Raichu)**: Bright, energetic lighting with tiny static sparks.
